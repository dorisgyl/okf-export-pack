# Suganthan Mohanadasan on OKF

Suganthan Mohanadasan (Norwegian entrepreneur, 20+ years in SEO, co-founder of Keyword Insights and Snippet Digital) published a detailed blog post framing Google's Open Knowledge Format as validation of bets the practitioner community had already placed. His central thesis: "When Google standardises a pattern you have already bet on, that is worth noticing, even at version 0.1." He had previously published a long post on making websites agent-ready — covering robots rules, llms.txt, schema, markdown negotiation, MCP cards — and sees OKF as Google turning one of those educated bets into a published standard. He positions OKF in a clear stack: llms.txt is a signpost, EntityMap is a who's who, and OKF is "the library itself."

Suganthan pointedly calls out the Dataplex-to-Knowledge-Catalog rebrand: "Google spent years selling Dataplex as enterprise data plumbing nobody outside a data team ever thought about. It is now Knowledge Catalog, described as an 'always-on context engine' for AI agents. The product barely moved. The positioning moved completely." He also debunks the GitHub repo's "not an official Google product" disclaimer, noting that Google puts the same boilerplate on most of its open-source repos (including its main AI samples repo) — it only means the code is unsupported, not that the format is unofficial. "Google wrote the spec and announced it. The boilerplate is just doing its usual lawyerly job." He built a free OKF generator tool and a WordPress plugin, turned his own site into a bundle, and compares the bet to schema markup: "Schema took the best part of a decade to pay off, and I am still glad I shipped it early. This is the same shape of bet, now with Google's name on it."
