Marie Haynes — SEO & AI consultant, founder of Antigravity Workspace CLI — called Google's Open Knowledge Format "really big news" in a cross-posted thread on X (328 likes, 7 replies) and LinkedIn (177 reactions, 1,030 comments). She described OKF as "a standardized way to store information in a directory of markdown files" that makes it "really easy to make a digital brain that agents can use." The files serve as a living wiki: agents can query, edit, and interlink them. She speculated OKF could replace tools like Notion or Obsidian for knowledge management, noting she could "think of so many uses for this."

Haynes immediately began building her own OKF library — starting with a personal project cataloging her pepper garden — and fed the SPEC.md and Google blog post to Antigravity to brainstorm integration ideas for her existing projects. She pointed her audience to the SPEC.md file as "an easier to understand explanation" than the blog post, and predicted that advanced AI agents (mentioning "Claude Fable 5") would "whip up some pretty amazing things based on this system." The strong engagement (1,000+ LinkedIn comments within days) suggests OKF resonated with the SEO and AI practitioner community as a practical, accessible standard.
