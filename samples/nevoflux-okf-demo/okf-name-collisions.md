# OKF Name Collisions

Google's **Open Knowledge Format (OKF)** v0.1 — a vendor-neutral spec for representing AI agent knowledge as markdown files with YAML frontmatter — shares the "OKF" acronym with at least two unrelated entities. First, **OKF-SCIS** (Open Knowledge Framework for Supply Chain Interoperability Specification) is a research project hosted by Helpful Engineering and developed in collaboration with NIST cyber-physical systems leaders, academics at Saint Joseph's University, University of New Mexico, University of Toulouse, and others. OKF-SCIS formalizes supply chain properties — resilience, security, compositionality — into a mathematical framework with computational methods including AI. It is published under a Creative Commons BY-ND license and has no connection to Google's data-analytics-oriented OKF. The explainx.ai analysis of Google's OKF explicitly flags this name collision under "Open Questions," noting: "Unrelated 'OKF' supply-chain spec exists (OKF-SCIS); Google's OKF is data/analytics/agent knowledge."

Second, the **Open Knowledge Foundation (OKFN)**, founded in 2004 and headquartered with a global network of chapters, is a non-profit organization that promotes open data, open content, and open technology. OKFN maintains tools like CKAN (the world's leading open-source data portal platform), Frictionless Data, Open Data Commons licenses, and the Open Definition. Despite superficial naming overlap — "Open Knowledge" appears in both — OKFN focuses on open government data, democratic transparency, and digital public goods, while Google's OKF is a file format specification for AI agent memory. The two are entirely separate efforts with different scopes, governance, and communities.
